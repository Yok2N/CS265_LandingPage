FAQ Writing Procedure

How to create clear, consistent, and helpful FAQ documents

Procedure for Writing an FAQ

Purpose

This guide outlines a consistent process for creating FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions) documents. It ensures that all FAQs are clear, concise, and easy for readers to navigate.

Step 1: Define the Goal
  • Determine what specific problem the FAQ will address.
  • Keep it focused — one FAQ should cover one main topic.
  • Write a short purpose statement explaining why this FAQ is needed.
Step 2: Collect Key Questions
  • Gather questions commonly asked by users, teammates, or support staff.
  • Review emails, chat logs, or feedback forms for recurring issues.
  • Confirm answers with subject matter experts before writing.
Step 3: Write in a Clear and Simple Format
  • Use plain language that anyone can understand.
  • Phrase questions as users would ask them (“How do I...?”).
  • Keep answers short, direct, and actionable.
Step 4: Structure the Document
  • Title: Make it descriptive and easy to recognize.
  • Purpose: Briefly explain what the FAQ covers.
  • Questions: Organize from most common to least common.
  • Procedures: Present actions in clear, numbered steps.
  • Tips: Include references, cautions, or related links.
Step 5: Review and Test
  • Have a new reader test whether the FAQ is easy to follow.
  • Verify that the questions are answered accurately and clearly.
  • Edit for tone, consistency, and simplicity.
Step 6: Maintain and Update
  • Set a review schedule (quarterly or annually).
  • Update when new questions arise or processes change.
  • Add a date/version at the end of each document for tracking.
Example FAQ Entry

Q: How often should this FAQ be reviewed?
A: At least once per quarter, or whenever the process changes or new questions appear.
